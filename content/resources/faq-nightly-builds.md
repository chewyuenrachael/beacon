# FAQ — Nightly Builds vs Stable

## What is the difference?

**Stable** releases are the default download. They are intended for day-to-day coursework and events where you need predictable behavior and fewer moving parts.

**Nightly** (or preview) channels ship newer features faster. They can be ideal for power users and demos of bleeding-edge capabilities—but they may also regress in ways that confuse first-time installers.

## What should I tell new students at an event?

Point them to **stable** unless they explicitly want to try experimental features. Add one line to your slide footer: “Having install issues? Switch to stable and retry.”

## What should power users use?

Nightly can be appropriate for club officers, hackathon regulars, or research labs that already tolerate occasional breakage. Encourage them to **report bugs** with version numbers and repro steps rather than assuming “it’s magic.”

## How do I explain updates without FOMO?

Frame it like any other dev tool: “You’re not behind if you’re on stable—you’re on the supported path.” Nightly is optional acceleration, not a requirement to be a “real” user.

## Campus-specific tip

If your school’s network or antivirus quarantines new binaries, stable builds are sometimes allow-listed sooner. When in doubt, test both channels on **school Wi‑Fi** the day before a workshop.
