# Hackathon Sponsorship Playbook

Hackathons compress attention into one weekend. Your job is to turn that spike into **lasting adoption**: students who open Cursor on Monday for coursework, not only for the prize track.

## Sponsorship tiers (conceptual)

Work with org committees early; align to their budget and MLH / student-run norms.

- **API / credits partner:** Lowest touch—logo, table, judging. Good for brand, weak for depth.
- **Workshop sponsor:** 45‑minute workshop + office hours block. **Preferred minimum** for Cursor.
- **Track sponsor:** Prize for “best use of AI-assisted development” with a rubric that rewards **thoughtful use** (tests, docs, safety), not vibe-coded spaghetti.

Always ask for: **workshop slot**, **Discord moderator role**, **table power strips**, and **judging brief** 48 hours before demos.

## Booth and run-of-show

- **Friday evening:** Setup table, poster with QR to student discount + workshop signup. Do **not** hard-sell—be the helpful table where people fix `git` and env issues.
- **Saturday mid-day:** Run the workshop (live build: API + small UI + tests). Record screen for async.
- **Saturday night:** Office hours near the snack line—high traffic, low pressure.
- **Sunday demos:** Send one ambassador to **judging prep** to explain the track criteria to judges (reduces “they just used ChatGPT” dismissals).

## Student messaging

- Lead with **workflow**: “Ship faster on your stack with inline edits and repo-wide refactors.”
- Address **academic integrity** head-on: Cursor is a tool; course policies still apply—see campus FAQ.
- Offer **office hours** for teams stuck on deployment, not just “prompt ideas.”

## Handoff after the event

Within 48 hours: post **workshop repo**, **recording link**, and **discount reminder** in hackathon Discord. Tag org leads with a thank-you and one metric you can share (e.g., workshop attendance count).
