# Cafe Cursor — Campus Ambassador Runbook

Cafe Cursor is a low-friction way to meet builders where they already hang out: over coffee, with laptops open. The goal is not a formal talk—it is **trust, demos, and clear next steps** (install Cursor, join your campus channel, show up at the next workshop).

## Venue selection criteria

Pick a place students already use for studying and casual meetups—not a loud bar or a silent library zone.

- **Seating:** Prefer 8–20 seats in one cluster, or adjacent tables you can reserve or hold with friendly signage. Everyone should be able to plug in.
- **Wi‑Fi:** Test upload/download the day before. If the network is flaky, bring a phone hotspot backup and post the password on a single printed sheet.
- **Noise:** Background buzz is fine; screaming espresso grinders are not. Sit away from the speaker if the cafe pipes loud music.
- **Policy:** Confirm with staff that laptops and a small group are OK for 90 minutes. Tip well and leave the table cleaner than you found it.
- **Access:** Ground floor or elevator-accessible when possible so students with mobility needs do not self-exclude.

## RSVP timing

- **T‑14 days:** Save the date in Discord/Slack/GroupMe with a one-line pitch (“Free coffee + Cursor office hours—bring your bug”).
- **T‑7 days:** Post a concrete agenda (15 min intro, 45 min coworking + demos, 15 min Q&A) and **require RSVP** (Google Form or Discord reaction) so you know how much swag to pack.
- **T‑48 hours:** Reminder with location pin, Wi‑Fi notes, and “what to bring” (laptop + one real task: homework repo, LeetCode, or a side project).
- **T‑2 hours:** Final bump for stragglers; pin the thread.

Target **10–25 RSVPs** for a first run; smaller is OK if engagement is high.

## Swag list (per 20 attendees)

- 20 die-cut stickers (Cursor + campus chapter if you have one)
- 10–15 **notebook / notepad** units (cheap, always taken)
- 5–10 **metal pins or keycaps** for volunteers who help others install
- Printed **one-pagers:** student discount URL, honor-code-safe usage tips, link to nightly vs stable (see FAQ)
- Optional: **$5 coffee gift cards** for 3 “best question” or “first successful Agent fix” winners

Keep a small **sign-in sheet** or QR to your Typeform for “I was here” (helps attribution without being creepy).

## Coffee shop pitch template (30 seconds)

> “Hey—I’m [name], Cursor campus ambassador at [school]. We’re doing informal office hours here today: grab a drink, open your laptop, and we’ll help you set up Cursor on a real assignment—not a toy demo. If you’re already using it, bring a gnarly refactor or test you want to speed up. Totally free, no recruiting pitch—just tools and workflows that save you time.”

If someone is hesitant: offer **5 minutes** to watch you fix *their* file, not yours.

## Day-of timeline (90 minutes)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0:00 | Arrive early, test Wi‑Fi, lay out swag, put up a small table tent: “Cursor office hours.” |
| 0:15 | Welcome, land norms: headphones OK, share screens only if you want, photos opt-in. |
| 0:25 | 10‑minute live demo: one real bugfix with Cmd+K / Agent, show diff + test run. |
| 0:35 | Coworking: rotate—**pair students with ambassadors** by stack (Python, JS, Java). |
| 1:05 | Lightning round: 3 students share what they shipped in 25 minutes. |
| 1:15 | Close loop: discount link, next event date, Discord invite, thank the cafe staff. |

## Common failure modes (and fixes)

- **“I can’t install Cursor on this machine.”** Keep one loaner laptop or a cloud IDE screenshot deck as backup; still walk them through account creation and discount on their phone.
- **“The model won’t see my private repo.”** Remind them to open the **folder**, not a single file, and to trust the workspace once. Never ask them to paste SSH keys into chat.
- **Low turnout:** Pair with another club’s announcement, move to a 30‑minute “micro session” the same week, or offer **1:1 slots** in the follow-up email so shy attendees still convert.
